Route Purchase Agreement

This Route Purchase Agreement is made on June 16, 2026, between Entrusted Lawncare (owner: Tyler Bonahoom) ("Seller") and __________________________ ("Buyer").

1. What is being sold

Seller sells, and Buyer buys, Seller's active recurring lawn-care customer route — the customers listed in Exhibit A (the "Customers"), along with their service history and Seller's goodwill toward them.

2. Also included — bonus past-customer contacts

As a courtesy, Seller also hands over its list of past and one-time customer contacts as potential leads for the Buyer. These are not active recurring clients, carry no guarantee, and are not part of the Price or the per-customer values in Exhibit A. After the sale, Seller will not contact them.

3. What is NOT included

This is a route-only sale. Excluded: the "Entrusted Lawncare" name and brand, the business phone number and email, the website and social accounts, and all vehicles, equipment, and tools.

4. Price

Total price: $11,000, payable at closing.

5. Per-customer value & 60-day retention credit

Each Customer is assigned a value in Exhibit A (the values add up to the Price). A portion of the Price equal to $2,750 (the "Holdback") is held back for 60 days after closing. If any listed Customer cancels or declines service within those 60 days for reasons other than the Buyer's own act or omission, that Customer's Exhibit A value is credited/refunded to the Buyer from the Holdback. Whatever Holdback remains is released to Seller at day 61.

6. No guarantee of continuation

The Customers are at-will / verbal clients — there are no written service contracts. Seller will send each Customer one friendly introduction giving the Buyer a good word (see the Transfer Plan). Whether each Customer continues, and the day, service, and price going forward, is worked out directly between the Customer and the Buyer's company. Seller makes no promise that any Customer will keep the same schedule, service, or price — Seller's only retention commitment is the 60-day credit in Section 5.

7. Seller will not come back for them (non-solicit, plain English)

After the sale, Tyler Bonahoom will not contact these Customers to win their lawn-care business back, and will not run a competing lawn service that targets them. Once they are sold, Seller leaves them to the Buyer. (There is no broad non-compete — Seller is not otherwise restricted.)

8. Transition help

Seller will send the introduction message to every Customer, answer reasonable questions for up to 2 weeks, and offer one optional route ride-along if the Buyer wants it. The ride-along is a courtesy, not a requirement.

9. General

This is the entire agreement, governed by the laws of the State of Minnesota, and may be changed only in writing signed by both parties.

Transfer Plan & Hand-off

Keeping this simple — here's exactly how the hand-off works.

The hand-off

  1. At closing, Tyler Bonahoom sends each customer a short message letting them know the route has changed hands and giving the Buyer a good word (script below).
  2. If a customer wants to continue, the Buyer takes it from there — the Buyer's company works out the day, service, and price directly with each customer.
  3. Optional ride-along. If the Buyer would like, Tyler Bonahoom will do one drive-through of the route as a courtesy. It's optional and not a big deal — there are no gate codes or special access needed.

That's it. No drawn-out transition.

Customer announcement (from Tyler Bonahoom — text or email)

Hi [First name], quick note — I'm moving on to focus on another business, so I've handed my lawn-care route to [Buyer name]. I trust them and wanted to give you a good word. They'll reach out to introduce themselves; anything going forward (schedule, service, pricing) you'll set up directly with them. Thank you for letting me take care of your lawn these past few seasons! — Tyler Bonahoom

Buyer introduction (from the Buyer — text or email)

Hi [First name], I'm [Buyer name] — Tyler Bonahoom passed along your lawn service to me and gave you a great recommendation. I'd love to keep your lawn looking sharp this season. Let's set up the details that work for you — reach me any time at [phone].

Note

Because these are at-will customers, continuation isn't guaranteed — but Tyler Bonahoom's good word plus a prompt, friendly intro from the Buyer is the best possible start.
